Favorite Destinations

Dolomites, Italy

The Dolomites are a mountain range located in northeastern Italy. The Dolomites, known as the "Pale Mountains", take their name from the carbonate rock dolomite. Mount Cook (Aoraki), Caterbury, New Zealand

Mount Cook (Aoraki) is the highest mountain in New Zealand at 3,724 metres (12,218 feet). It lies in the Southern Alps, the mountain range which runs the length of the South Island. The mountain range is a popular tourist destination and a favourite challenge for mountain climbers.

The Turquoise Lake, Altai Mountains

The Altai Mountains are a mountain range in Central and East Asia. The name comes from the two words al that means "gold/reddish/yellowish" in Mongolic language and the -tai word that means "mountain" in Turkic languages too; thus, literally, the "Golden Mountain".

The Torres del Paine National Park, Patagonia, Chile

The Torres del Paine National Park is located in Patagonia, Chile in southern region of South America. The Torres del Paine (Spanish for "Towers of Paine", "Paine" being the old indigenous name for the colour blue), three immense rock towers give the park its name.
